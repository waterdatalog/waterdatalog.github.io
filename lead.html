What is Lead?

Lead is the 82nd element of the periodic table. Lead was a metal used in new pipes and plumbing systems until 1986. However many of these pipes remain as lead pipes and have not been updated. (Lead-Contaminated Water.”)

How does lead get into the water?

Pipes that are made of lead corrode after time. Small bits of the pipe break break off and enter the water stream. Which can then either go right to the sink tap, or out into lakes, rivers and the ocean. (Lead-Contaminated Water.”)
